Insurance & Water Damage

home blog
Water DamageSan Angelo, TX

One of the first questions San Angelo homeowners ask after a leak is whether insurance will help. The honest answer: it depends on the source, how sudden it was, and which endorsements you carry.

Often covered: sudden and accidental water

Many HO-3 policies cover sudden failures — a supply line that bursts, a water heater that ruptures, or an ice-maker line that lets go. Coverage usually aims at resulting damage to the dwelling and, depending on the policy, personal property.

Often excluded or limited

  • Flooding from outside — rising water and many storm-related exterior floods need a separate flood policy
  • Long-term seepage or neglect — a slow leak you ignored for months is frequently denied
  • Sewer backup — often needs a specific endorsement
  • Poor maintenance — failures tied to deferred upkeep can be disputed

What helps your claim

Stop the source if safe, document thoroughly, and get professional drying started. Moisture logs and photos from restoration pros support the scope. We're not your insurer and can't promise coverage — but we can connect you with local pros who document losses clearly.
