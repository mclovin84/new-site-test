Freeze Pipe Prevention

home blog
Water DamageSan Angelo, TX

San Angelo doesn't get endless winters — but when Arctic air drops hard, pipes in garages, crawlspaces, exterior walls and irrigation risers can freeze and split. The thaw is when many homeowners discover the flood.

Before the freeze

  • Disconnect and drain outdoor hoses; insulate outdoor faucets
  • Open cabinet doors under sinks on exterior walls so warm air reaches pipes
  • Know your main shutoff location before you need it
  • Let a trickle of water run on the coldest nights for vulnerable lines
  • Keep the thermostat set — don't drastically cut heat while you're away

If a pipe bursts

Shut the main water off if you can do so safely, avoid electrical hazards, document the damage, and call for extraction. Standing water from a freeze break can soak multiple rooms quickly.

After the emergency

Ask the plumber to repair the break, then make sure structural drying is completed — not just mopping. Hidden moisture in walls after a freeze flood is a common mold starter.
