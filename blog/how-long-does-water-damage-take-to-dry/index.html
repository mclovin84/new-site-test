Drying Timelines

home blog
Water DamageSan Angelo, TX

Homeowners often hope a fan and open windows will dry a wet room overnight. In reality, professional structural drying for a typical residential loss often runs about 3–5 days of continuous equipment — sometimes longer.

What the timeline usually looks like

  • Day 0: extract standing water, set air movers and dehumidifiers
  • Days 1–3: daily moisture readings; equipment adjusted as humidity falls
  • Days 3–5+: materials reach dry standards; equipment removed; repairs begin

What slows drying in San Angelo homes

Hardwood floors, multiple layers of flooring, wet insulation, cabinet toe-kicks, and concrete slabs all hold moisture longer. Storm losses with wet attics add more mass to dry. High outdoor humidity during monsoon season can also make dehumidification work harder.

Why "dry to the touch" isn't enough

Surfaces can feel dry while framing or subfloors still read wet. Pros use meters so they know when to stop — protecting you from a surprise mold smell weeks later.
