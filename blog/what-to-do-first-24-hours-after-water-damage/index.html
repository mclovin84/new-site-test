First 24 Hours

home blog
Water DamageSan Angelo, TX

When water shows up where it shouldn't in a San Angelo home, the first day sets the ceiling on how bad the loss gets. Mold can begin within 24–48 hours, and every hour of standing water means more material absorbing moisture.

1. Stay safe

Avoid standing water near outlets or appliances. If you can shut off the water source safely, do it. Kill power to wet rooms only if you can reach the breaker dry-footed. If sewage or outdoor flood water is involved, keep kids and pets out and minimize contact.

2. Document before you move everything

Photograph and short-video every wet room, the source if visible, and damaged belongings. Insurers often want a clear before-cleanup record. Don't throw items away until your carrier says it's okay unless they're a health hazard.

3. Limit spread

Move dry furniture and rugs out of the wet zone. Hang wet drapes if they're salvageable. Don't run your household vacuum as a water extractor — it can fail and create a shock risk. A wet/dry vac on clean water only is a stopgap, not a substitute for professional extraction.

4. Call for extraction and drying

Local restoration pros bring truck-mounted or portable extractors, air movers and dehumidifiers, plus meters to find moisture inside walls. Call (000) 000-0000 anytime — nights and weekends included — and we'll connect you for a free estimate.

5. Start the insurance conversation

Report the claim promptly if you have coverage. Keep a simple log of who you spoke with and what was said. Pros can share moisture readings and scopes that help your adjuster understand the job.
