What to Do in the First 24 Hours After Water Damage

The actions you take in the first day often decide whether this is a minor cleanup or a major renovation.

Discovering water where it shouldn't be — pooling on the kitchen floor, dripping through the ceiling, soaking into the carpet — is stressful. But the first 24 hours are also when you have the most control over how bad it gets. Here's exactly what to do.

1. Make sure it's safe

Before anything else, think safety. Water and electricity are a dangerous mix. If water is near outlets, appliances or your electrical panel — and you can reach the breaker safely — shut off power to the affected area. If you can't reach it without stepping into water, stay out and call an electrician or your utility. Watch for slip hazards and, in the case of a sewage backup or flood water, avoid contact entirely; that water can carry bacteria.

2. Stop the source of the water

If the water is coming from your plumbing, shut it off. For a specific fixture, use its local shutoff valve; for a bigger problem, use your home's main water shutoff (often near where the line enters the house or by the water meter). Knowing where that main valve is before an emergency is one of the best things a San Angelo homeowner can do. If the source is a roof leak from a storm, place buckets and, if it's safe, cover the area — but don't climb onto a wet or damaged roof.

3. Call for professional help early

Call a restoration pro as soon as the situation is safe and the source is stopped. This isn't about upselling — it's timing. Professional extraction and drying started on day one can save flooring, drywall and trim that would otherwise have to be removed. Our line is answered 24/7, and the estimate is free, so there's no downside to calling early. Reach us here or call (000) 000-0000.

4. Document everything for insurance

Before you start moving things or cleaning up, take photos and video of all the damage — the water, the source if visible, and every affected room and item. This documentation is gold when you file your claim. Keep any damaged items until your adjuster has seen them or given the okay to dispose of them, and save receipts for anything you buy during the emergency. For more, read our guide to water damage insurance in Texas.

5. Start limiting the damage (safely)

Once you've documented the scene, you can take some safe first steps while you wait for the pro:

  • Mop or blot up standing water and soak up what you can with towels.
  • Move rugs, furniture and valuables to a dry area. Put foil or wood blocks under furniture legs to prevent staining.
  • Lift and hang draperies and fabrics away from wet floors.
  • Open cabinet doors and drawers to help air circulate.
  • Remove and prop up wet cushions in a dry spot.

Don't use your household vacuum to remove water, don't use ceiling fixtures if the ceiling is wet, and don't enter rooms with standing water and live electricity.

6. Watch for the 24–48 hour mold window

Mold can begin to grow within a day or two on damp materials. That's the single biggest reason not to "wait and see" — even if things look like they're drying on their own, hidden moisture in walls and subfloor keeps feeding potential mold. Proper structural drying is what actually removes that risk.

The bottom line

Stay safe, stop the water, document it, and call for professional help fast. Do those four things and you've given yourself the best possible outcome.
