Don't Wait It Out

Signs You Need Water Damage Restoration

Water damage often hides behind walls and under floors long after the visible puddle is gone. If you notice any of these, it's worth a free inspection:

  • Staining, bubbling or discoloration on ceilings and walls
  • A musty, damp smell that won't go away
  • Warped, cupping or soft spots in wood or laminate flooring
  • Peeling paint, blistered baseboards or swollen drywall
  • A spike in your water bill with no obvious cause (possible slab leak)
  • Visible mold spots or dark grout lines in bathrooms
  • Popping or crackling sounds in the slab or walls

Good to Know

Water Damage FAQ

Straight answers to the questions San Angelo homeowners ask us most.

How soon should I call after water damage happens?

Immediately. Water spreads within minutes and mold can begin to grow in as little as 24–48 hours. The faster water is extracted and drying begins, the less of your home is affected — and the lower the cost. Even overnight, call so a pro can start the clock on drying.

Do you work with my insurance company?

Yes. While every policy is different, the pros we connect you with document the loss thoroughly — photos, moisture readings and an itemized scope — so you have what you need to file. We can also speak with your adjuster to explain the work that's required.

How long does it take to dry out water damage?

Most residential jobs dry in about 3–5 days with commercial air movers and dehumidifiers running continuously, though dense materials like hardwood or a wet slab can take longer. Daily moisture readings confirm when your structure is truly dry.

Should wet drywall and flooring be replaced?

Not always. Drywall that's only damp can often be dried in place; drywall that's saturated, contaminated or has swelled usually needs removal. The same goes for flooring — the pro will test moisture levels and recommend the least invasive option that still fully dries the structure.

Does water damage get worse if I wait?

Yes, quickly. Standing water migrates into framing and subfloor, adhesives fail, and microbial growth sets in. What starts as a small, insurable loss can become a major mold and structural problem within days.

Are you available at night and on weekends?

Yes. Our line is answered 24/7, including nights, weekends and holidays, because that's often when pipe bursts and storms happen.

What does water damage restoration cost in San Angelo?

It depends on how much water, what it touched and whether it was clean or contaminated. That's exactly why the estimate is free — a pro will inspect, measure and give you a clear number before any work starts. See our insurance guide for how coverage affects your out-of-pocket cost.
