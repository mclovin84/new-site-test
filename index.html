signs you need help

Water damage often hides behind walls and under floors long after the visible puddle is gone.

  • Staining, bubbling or discoloration on ceilings and walls
  • A musty, damp smell that won't go away
  • Warped, cupping or soft spots in wood or laminate flooring
  • Peeling paint, blistered baseboards or swollen drywall
  • A spike in your water bill with no obvious cause (possible slab leak)
  • Visible mold spots or dark grout lines in bathrooms
  • Popping or crackling sounds in the slab or walls

the process

A clear sequence the local pros follow to dry your property the right way — not just the fast way.

1. Inspect & Assess

Moisture meters and thermal imaging map how far the water has traveled — including inside walls and slabs.

2. Extract Water

3. Dry & Dehumidify

4. Clean & Restore

water damage faq

Straight answers to the questions San Angelo homeowners ask most.

  • How soon should I call after water damage happens?

    Immediately. Water spreads within minutes and mold can begin to grow in as little as 24–48 hours. The faster water is extracted and drying begins, the less of your home is affected — and the lower the cost.

  • Do you work with my insurance company?

    Yes. While every policy is different, the pros we connect you with document the loss thoroughly — photos, moisture readings and an itemized scope — so you have what you need to file.

  • How long does it take to dry out water damage?

    Most residential jobs dry in about 3–5 days with commercial air movers and dehumidifiers running continuously, though dense materials like hardwood or a wet slab can take longer.

  • Should wet drywall and flooring be replaced?

    Not always. Drywall that's only damp can often be dried in place; saturated or contaminated materials usually need removal. Pros test moisture levels and recommend the least invasive option that still fully dries the structure.

  • Does water damage get worse if I wait?

    Yes, quickly. Standing water migrates into framing and subfloor, adhesives fail, and microbial growth sets in. What starts as a small loss can become a major mold and structural problem within days.

  • Are you available at night and on weekends?

    Yes. Our line is answered 24/7, including nights, weekends and holidays, because that's often when pipe bursts and storms happen.

  • What does water damage restoration cost in San Angelo?

    It depends on how much water, what it touched and whether it was clean or contaminated. That's why the estimate is free — a pro will inspect, measure and give you a clear number before work starts.
