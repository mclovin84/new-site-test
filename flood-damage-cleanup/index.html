Flood Damage Cleanup in San Angelo, TX

West Texas storms can flood a property in minutes. Vetted local pros remove flood water and mud, sanitize what's contaminated, and dry the structure so your home or business recovers safely.

San Angelo sits in the heart of flash-flood country. When a slow-moving thunderstorm parks over the Concho Valley, dry draws and streets can turn into fast-moving water in a matter of minutes — and that water finds its way into homes and businesses.

Flood cleanup is different from a clean-water leak. Storm and flood water is almost always contaminated — it picks up soil, road grime, fertilizer, and whatever it flows across on the way in. That makes it a health hazard as well as a structural one, and it changes how the cleanup has to be done.

What flood cleanup involves

  • Water and muck removal — pumping out flood water and shoveling the silt and debris it leaves behind.
  • Removal of unsalvageable materials — flood-soaked carpet, pad, and often the lower section of drywall and insulation are removed for safety.
  • Cleaning & disinfection — hard surfaces are washed and treated with antimicrobials to kill bacteria and mold spores.
  • Structural drying — air movers and dehumidifiers dry the framing and subfloor to normal levels.
  • Deodorizing — removing the musty, muddy smell floodwater leaves in a building.

Why flood water can't just be dried in place

Because flood water is Category 3 "black water," porous materials that soaked it up generally can't be sanitized well enough to keep. Trying to simply dry a flooded carpet or wet drywall in place risks leaving bacteria and mold behind. A trained pro knows exactly what has to go and what can be saved — protecting your family or customers while still keeping the job as economical as possible.

Act fast, stay safe

If flooding is active, never walk into standing water that may be in contact with electrical outlets or appliances, and don't use gas or electricity in a flooded area until it's been checked. Once you're safe, call — the sooner cleanup begins after the water recedes, the more of your property can be saved and the lower the chance of long-term mold. See our mold remediation and storm damage pages for related help.

Insurance note

Standard homeowners insurance in Texas usually does not cover rising-water flooding — that typically requires separate flood insurance. However, storm water that enters through a wind- or hail-damaged roof may be covered. The pros document the source and damage carefully so you have the evidence your insurer needs.
