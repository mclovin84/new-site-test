Water Damage Restoration in San Angelo, TX

Water damage restoration is the full process of returning a property to its pre-loss condition after water intrusion — not just pumping out the puddle. Done properly, it protects your home's structure, your indoor air quality and your wallet.

In San Angelo, the water we deal with comes from every direction. A summer thunderstorm dumps two inches of rain in an hour and overwhelms a roof or clogged gutter. A hard freeze — the kind that shut down West Texas during Winter Storm Uri — splits a pipe in an exterior wall. Years of the Concho Valley's notoriously hard water finally corrode a water heater until it lets go. Or a slab leak quietly saturates flooring for weeks before anyone notices the bill. Whatever the source, the response is the same: act fast, dry thoroughly, verify with instruments.

What full restoration includes

When you call, a local pro will walk your property with moisture meters and, where needed, thermal imaging to find every place the water has traveled — including behind walls and under flooring where it isn't visible. From there, restoration typically covers:

  • Emergency water extraction — removing standing water with pumps and extractors before it soaks deeper.
  • Structural drying — air movers and dehumidifiers dry framing, subfloor, drywall and insulation to normal moisture levels.
  • Controlled demolition — only when materials are too saturated or contaminated to save, kept to the minimum needed.
  • Cleaning & sanitizing — antimicrobial treatment to discourage mold and remove odors.
  • Repairs & reconstruction — replacing drywall, baseboards, flooring and paint to finish the job.

Why speed matters so much

Water damage is progressive. In the first minutes, water spreads across floors and wicks up walls. Within hours, furniture finishes bleed, drywall swells and metal begins to tarnish. By 24 to 48 hours, mold can start to colonize damp materials and that musty smell sets in. Wait a week and you're often looking at warped hardwood, delaminated subfloor and a mold problem that turns a modest, insurable claim into a major renovation. Calling early is the single biggest thing you can do to control both damage and cost.

The three categories of water

Not all water is equal, and the category drives the response:

  • Category 1 (clean): from a supply line or water heater. Lowest risk, but it degrades to Category 2 within a day or two.
  • Category 2 (gray): from appliances like dishwashers or washing machines, containing some contaminants.
  • Category 3 (black): sewage, flood water or anything grossly contaminated. Requires strict safety precautions and disposal of most porous materials.

A trained pro identifies the category first, because it determines what can be dried and saved versus what must be removed. Learn more on our sewage cleanup and flood damage pages.

Working with your insurance

Most sudden, accidental water losses — a burst pipe, a failed water heater, a storm-driven roof leak — are covered by standard homeowners policies in Texas, while gradual leaks and flooding from rising water usually are not. The pros we connect you with document everything from day one: photos, an itemized scope and daily moisture logs. That record is what makes the difference when your adjuster reviews the claim.
