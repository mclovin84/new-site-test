Structural Drying & Dehumidification in San Angelo, TX

The step that actually prevents long-term damage. Commercial air movers and dehumidifiers pull moisture out of your building's structure — and instruments confirm when it's truly dry.

Extracting the standing water is only half the battle. The moisture that soaks into drywall, subfloor, framing and insulation is what causes warping, delamination and mold — and it can't be mopped up. That's what structural drying is for.

Structural drying uses purpose-built equipment and building science to remove bound moisture from materials and the surrounding air, bringing everything back to a normal, stable moisture content. Done right, it saves materials that would otherwise have to be torn out and replaced.

The equipment behind it

  • Air movers — high-velocity fans that sweep moisture off surfaces and into the air where it can be removed.
  • Commercial dehumidifiers — LGR (low-grain refrigerant) or desiccant units that pull large volumes of water vapor out of the air.
  • Specialty drying systems — injection/mat systems for hardwood floors and cavity drying for wall interiors.
  • Moisture meters & thermal imaging — the instruments that guide the whole process and prove it worked.

Why "dry to the touch" isn't good enough

A wall can feel dry on the surface while framing behind it still holds damaging moisture. That trapped moisture is exactly what feeds mold and rots wood. The pros we connect you with take daily moisture readings and document them, drying until materials reach a verified dry standard — not just until they look and feel okay. This is also the record your insurer wants to see.

How long does it take?

Most homes dry in about three to five days of continuous operation, though the timeline depends on how much water there was, what materials are involved and how quickly drying started. Dense materials — hardwood, plaster, a wet concrete slab — take longer. The equipment runs around the clock; the sooner it's set up, the faster and more completely your property dries.

A note on West Texas air

San Angelo's typically low outdoor humidity can help drying — but only with proper equipment and containment. Simply opening windows is unreliable and can even make things worse when it's humid out or during a storm. Controlled, dehumidified drying is consistent and measurable, which is why it's the professional standard. Structural drying is a core part of water damage restoration and the best defense against mold.
