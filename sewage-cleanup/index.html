Sewage Cleanup in San Angelo, TX

Sewage backups are a genuine health hazard, not just a mess. Vetted local pros contain the contamination, remove and dispose of it safely, then disinfect and dry the affected area.

Sewage is classified as Category 3 "black water" — the most hazardous type of water intrusion. It carries bacteria, viruses and other pathogens, which is why sewage cleanup is a job for trained pros with proper protective equipment, not a mop and bucket.

In San Angelo, sewage problems tend to come from a few sources: a main line clogged by roots or grease, an overwhelmed municipal system during heavy rain, a failed lift pump, or a simple toilet overflow that turns out to be more than clean water. Whatever the cause, the response has to prioritize safety.

What safe sewage cleanup involves

  • Containment — sealing off the area so contaminants and odors don't spread through the building.
  • Extraction & disposal — removing the sewage and contaminated water for proper disposal.
  • Removal of porous materials — carpet, pad, saturated drywall and other porous items that contacted black water are removed.
  • Cleaning & disinfection — hard surfaces are scrubbed and treated with hospital-grade antimicrobials.
  • Drying & deodorizing — the structure is dried and treated to eliminate lingering odor.

Why you shouldn't clean it yourself

Beyond the obvious unpleasantness, sewage exposure can cause real illness through skin contact, inhalation of aerosols, or cross-contamination of other areas of your home. Porous materials that soak up black water usually can't be disinfected reliably and need to be removed — a judgment call the pros make so your family or staff aren't left with a lingering biohazard.

Fast, discreet, thorough

The local pros we connect you with treat sewage jobs with urgency and discretion, working to make the space safe and livable again as quickly as possible. If the backup also caused broader water damage, they'll coordinate structural drying and, where needed, mold remediation as part of the same job. Available 24/7 across San Angelo and the Concho Valley.

Insurance

Many Texas homeowners policies cover sudden sewer backups only if a specific backup endorsement was added — it's worth checking your policy. The pros document everything thoroughly to support whatever coverage you have. See our insurance guide.
